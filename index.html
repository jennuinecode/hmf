THE MISSION

I’m here to give you the gloves, it’s going to be up to you to throw the punches.

I will give you the tools you need to work hard in the gym and build your physique. I will set you up with the cardio regimen and macro profile that you need in order to achieve your goals. I will provide you with education and tips in every way that I possible can.

I will teach you, lead you and guide you. But one thing I cannot do, is do the work for you, nor would I want to. There is so much growth that can and will happen from you putting yourself to the test. There are lessons that you would never learn if I held your hand in the gym or tracked all of your food for you. I could spoon feed you, but how would you learn to hold your own that way?

THE NAME CHANGE

Body By Heidi No More...

Many of you here are probably wondering “Why the switch?” (or maybe you’re not, who knows). So I figured I would explain. When I started Body by Heidi in 2013, the name to me was just catchy. I tossed the idea around for awhile and Body by Heidi just seemed to roll off the tongue, so I ran with it.

Over the years, I can’t tell you the amount of times I’ve had clients use statements like “Look what YOU did!” “Look what YOU built!”, when sending in their progress photos. And every single time I heard something like that, it made me cringe. Why am I getting all of this credit? Why is the first person they thank ME? They did all the work. They got up out of bed, hit the gym, planned their day, tracked their food, got on the treadmill when they didn’t want to. Sure, I helped them by providing them the tools they needed. I was there to support them when they were feeling down. But they put in the work, not me.

I’ve been so lucky to be a part of their journeys. If you’ve come across any of my clients over the years, you’ve seen what they have accomplished, it’s amazing. If you want to hand out credit, it needs to go to them. None of the changes they made were by me. Their bodies are not mine and I did not build them. Their bodies are their own. The transformations they’ve made, inside and out, are from their own sweat, hard work and commitment. Their bodies are not “by Heidi” and I want all of them, as well as any future clients who come to me to know that.

Body by Heidi was (and is) my baby. It was one of the first HUGE leaps of faith I ever took in my life. It’s my first business and it will always hold that special place, but it was time to evolve. So I introduce to you: Heidi Marie Fitness.

Formerly known as Body by Heidi, launched in 2013 as nothing more than a hope to do what I could in order to help other others achieve their goals, my online coaching business has grown like nothing I could have ever imagined.
Starting out I never had the intention of leaving my full-time job to work the greater part of my career from home, but here we are. Coaching people from all over the world has been one of the most exciting and rewarding things I’ve ever been able to do. I never knew the ability I had to connect with so many people the way I have been able to as a coach. I hope to continue working hard to help as many people as possible become the very best versions of themselves and teaching them as much as I can along the way.

MY STORY

  • My interest in fitness began long before I ever even knew what the bodybuilding world was. I grew up being pretty athletic; I participated and competed in track & cheerleading throughout middle school and high school. When I got to college, I did the typical freshman thing and chose a major just because I had to (I started off in psychology). While I knew it was definitely something I was interested in, it didn’t feel like the right fit for something that I was going to carry on with for the rest of my life as a career. After some time and self-reflection, I finally changed majors and found myself in an Exercise Science program. With just a few weeks of classes under my belt, I knew I was in the right place.

    I graduated from Kent State University in 2011 with my Bachelor’s in Exercise Science. Fresh out of college I kind of felt like “Uhh, now what?” Like any broke college grad, I started the job hunt. Not long afterwards, I became a Personal Trainer and a Wellness Program Coordinator. I also went out on a limb and competed in my first NPC Bikini competition in April of 2013. Throughout the three years I spent in a corporate job, building my training clientele and experiencing the life of a competitor, I spent a lot of time learning and growing both as a fitness professional and a person. I felt a pull on my heart to expand my horizons and find a way to help even more people who were outside of my immediate reach.

    In 2013, my business, Body by Heidi was born. I left my full-time job and took a complete leap of faith in order to give my time and focus to my business. Fast forward a few years and here we are. Taking a chance and becoming a business owner was one of the biggest risks I’ve ever taken. Thankfully it has come with even bigger rewards. The amount of people I’ve been able to reach and impact has been overwhelming to say the least. I never imagined that I would have the opportunity to connect with so many people from all over the world and work with them in achieving their health and fitness goals.

    If you've followed my journey through social media, you know very well that I have had my fair share of wins, losses, setbacks and comebacks. It has not always been easy road for me, which is why I wanted to begin to help other girls achieve their fitness goals, but also teach them that we are all human. We all make mistakes and fall off track at times; no one is perfect. The key to success is to keep going no matter what. That's the exact attitude and determination I hope to instill in my clients. You can never lose if you are still trying, you only lose by giving up.

My online coaching programs are all uniquely designed to fit just what you’re looking for. Before even beginning to mold your program, I will consider your current dietary and training habits so that I know all I need to tailor your program so that it suits you best. We will talk about your goals, both immediate and long-term, and make sure that your program is customized in a way that will help you achieve those goals in the most efficient way possible.


NUTRITION

It’s part of my mission to break down the barrier of foods being viewed as “good” or “bad”.

There’s so much misinformation out there on nutrition. I want to play as much of a part as I can in debunking the myths and showing you that there’s a smarter, more sustainable way then cursing off entire food groups. We will approach the nutritional aspect of your plan by utilizing flexible dieting.

This isn’t about a quick fix. If that’s what you’re in search of, you’ve come to the wrong place. I will create a completely personalized macronutrient profile so that you are able to reach your goals in the healthiest and most maintainable way possible. I want you to learn that you can in fact enjoy foods you love, while reaching towards your goals. It’s about moderation, not deprivation.

CONTEST PREP

Interested in taking the stage? Let me help you shape your physique to bring your best...

All contest prep clients will go through an initial physique assessment in order to determine the appropriate length of time for prep. I prefer to take a 16 week minimum for competition prep, especially for anyone who has yet to take the stage for their first time. The more time we have to work with, the better. Contest prep is a huge undertaking and there’s no doubt there will be a few bumps along the road. Taking ample time to prep will allow us to make any adjustments needed as the weeks go on.

Your training and nutrition program will be customized to you and based around your current schedule and lifestyle. Our communication will be absolutely key throughout prep. As your coach, I will be here to support you in every way possible and do my very best to provide you with anything you may need, but I also expect you to meet the expectations I have for you.

Contest prep is a commitment to say the least. Are you committed?
